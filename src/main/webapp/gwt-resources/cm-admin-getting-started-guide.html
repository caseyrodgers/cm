Administrator Getting Started Guide

Help Whenever You Need It

This page is a quick reference for our reporting and management features. We also have an excellent 30 minute teacher video. For details, see our Teacher FAQ. But please, feel free to call your Account Manager at any time for phone support, a group webinar, or a personal training session.

Technical Requirements

Operation requires Flash (version 8 or later). Catchup Math should work with any modern browser on a full-sized PC or MAC from any broadband internet connection.

Quick Start

Check in Manage Groups for red-colored group names. Students go to catchupmath.com, enter your school Login Name and use the group name as the password. Students will be prompted to self-register and can start immediately on the associated program.

Select the Right Program for Each Student

Catchup Math will remediate and review the named subject. For example:

  • To prepare students for Algebra 1, select the Pre-Algebra Proficiency program.
  • For a weak student about to take Geometry, enroll them in Pre-Algebra Proficiency (they will be automatically advanced to Algebra 1 when they have successfully reviewed their Pre-Algebra learning gaps).
  • If you are not sure where to place a student, use the Auto-Enrollment program. The student will be given one or more quizzes to place them in the lowest program where they need help. Then, you can monitor their progress until they advance to the target level.
  • To concentrate on a specific topic (roughly correlated with the chapters in leading math textbooks), select a "Subject and Chapter" program. Then select a chapter topic from the drop-down menu.
  • To prepare for a graduation exam, choose one of our Graduation Prep programs.

Register Students

  • To manually register one or more students, use the Student Registration button.
  • To easily register multiple students, use the Self Registration option. You create a Group with the specific program for everyone in the group. Students can then log in with your school Login Name and the group name to create their own individual name and password.
  • To register students from an uploaded file, use the Bulk Registration option. You again create a Group with the specific program for everyone in the group, then upload a text file in tab separated format containing student names and their password.
  • To make any change to a registered student account, select the student and then use Edit Student.

Tell Students How to Log In

Tell students to go to catchupmath.com and then enter the school Login Name and their individual Password. Try this yourself with the jd12345 sample student.

Give Students a Brief Outline of How Catchup Math Works

Students will be given quizzes. Based on the problems they get wrong, the program will select one or more review topics. For each review topic, there are practice problems to solve, lessons to read, videos to watch, activities to try out. At a minimum, students must attempt the required practice problems or activities and receive a check-mark in order to proceed to the next topic. But students should be encouraged to try the other resources as well. Once the quiz has been passed and the topics reviewed, students are advanced to the next section of the program that covers new topics.

Tell Students How You Want Them to Use Catchup Math

Students should answer the quizzes carefully, working out their answers with pencil and paper or our whiteboard, so they only receive lessons they really need. You might give students a goal for their next session, such as work until you have passed at least two quizzes.

Best Practices in a Computer Lab

Visit each student, offer assistance as needed, and make sure the student is trying all the various learning resources. Also, review their progress on the spot by clicking the Help button and then clicking the Student History button.

How to Use the Show Work Feature

Students enter their own steps and answers on a virtual whiteboard by writing with the mouse. You can view their work at any time using the Student Detail History button.

Monitoring Student Progress

The Student Detail History button will present a reverse chronological history for a selected student. While viewing the history, select any line to view the lessons (standards) covered and those which the student was prescribed to review. Also, the entered "Show Work" may be viewed. There is a Report Card that summarizes student progress and effort including their prescribed lessons. The student summary page may be printed for the all registered students or for any selected group. The Student Detail History and Report Card may also be printed.

Assess Group Progress

Select students using the group-select and text-select from the summary page, then press Assessment to view charts of lessons most frequently used as well as general progress.

Custom Programs

This feature lets you assign any sequence of our lessons to students, without quizzes. Summary and detail reporting will monitor progress, and you can review student work on the whiteboard.

Program Details

Click the Program Details button to view the lesson topics correlated with each section of the Catchup Math programs.
